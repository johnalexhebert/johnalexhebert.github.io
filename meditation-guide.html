Meditation Guide for the Terminally Online Person

You are distracted.

Your attention is one of your most valuable assets you have.

Your attention is being stolen from you.

I will try to teach you how to get it back.

First, observe your mind with dispassion.

What does this mean?

This means to not react to thoughts that occur to you.

Consider thoughts as if they were leaves floating by in a river.

Acknowledge the thought, then let it go.

What is the lesson to be learned?

The lesson is that thoughts arise on their own.

Observe and learn how the mind cogitates.

Your thoughts arise from your body, from the accumulated set of sensations your body has experienced throughout its lifetime.

The human brain is not located just within the skull. It is spread throughout the body through the central nervous system.

All the sensations that a human being experiences shapes the brain by forming connections between synapses.

What is interesting is when experiences are sensed within the brain itself. How does a synapse connect in reaction to synapses connecting?

How does one record the experience of thinking about thinking?

Let us change the topic.

The task is to slow the constant stream of thoughts in order to learn how to focus on the most relevant thought.

This skill can take a long time to learn and master.

My advice is to practice silence.
